The human body is a source of thousands of types of bacteria and different parts of the body harbor different species. Bacteria found in the gut are not the same species that are found on a person's eyelashes, for example. Furthermore, between individuals the bacterial species may vary even in the same location. To test the hypothesis, navels of people across the country have been sampled to identify bacterial species that colonize our bellybuttons. To keep each person's identity is anonymous, they've been assigned an ID number. Select an individual ID below to see which bacterial species live in their navels.
